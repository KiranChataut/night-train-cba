Service 3's high operational costs from running double the service (day + night) eroded any advantage from avoiding depot fees. Service 1's minimal capital outlay made it the clear winner despite lower per-passenger comfort.

My contribution

What I worked on

Cost model

Built the full operational cost spreadsheet — personnel, energy, route pricing, station fees, track access charges, maintenance, inspection, and cleaning for all three service concepts. Used BVWP 2030 methodology and DB Netz AG TPS/APS price catalogs.

Benefits

Quantified indirect benefits — daytime travel-time savings, mode-shift travel-time savings, and a comfort benefit model based on a star-rating willingness-to-pay framework. Conducted literature review for all assumptions.

Methodology

Co-developed the CBA methodology section (Section 3.1) — NPV/BCR formulation, discount rate justification per EU Commission CBA Guide, and the framework for one-way sensitivity analysis.

Report

Wrote and formatted ~75% of the final report, including the Costs section (3.3), methodology overview, and all layout and document design.
